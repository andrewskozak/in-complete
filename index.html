We often think of the buildings that make up our urban landscape as being static objects, each with its own function. The ones that stand out are artifacts that become embedded in our culture and history.

This work started as an archive to document current restorations of these artifacts. When visiting these beautiful sites, I was frustrated with seeing them covered in scaffolding and fencing, which I found diminished the work underneath and coloured an otherwise pleasurable trip.

But actually, scaffolding tells us a lot about what’s going on underneath. Scaffolding is evidence that these buildings and artifacts are dynamic organisms that make up our cities. They are not built once and then forgotten, but maintained over time. We pass these artifacts along to the next generation because they are valuable. They have meaning that lies beyond simple brick and mortar. These buildings hold histories, cultures, and craftsmanship.

We look after them because we have decided that they hold value. We spend money, time, and effort on old buildings, paintings, sculptures, and archeological ruins because preserving them has become a core part of being human. We never met the people who made them, but they hold such significance that they endure generations. These artifacts hold a thread that binds us together.

On April 15th 2019, a fire broke out in the roof of the Notre-Dame Cathedral in Paris which destroyed its centuries old framework and 19th century spire. For years it was closed to the public while it was tirelessly restored by 2000 skilled artisans, carpenters, stonemasons, painters, and architects. 340,000 donors from 150 countries supported the effort financially.

The 860 year old cathedral reopened to visitors in December 2024. This work serves as a reminder that even if the aesthetic quality of these artifacts is diminished, scaffolding is a temporary structure that signify value underneath.

These are not objects for us to possess or capture for ourselves, but reminders of enduring human achievement that populates our cities and the responsibility we hold for the preservation of the past to be enjoyed by the future.
